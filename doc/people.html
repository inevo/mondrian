Who was Mondrian?

The mondrian project is named after the artist Piet Mondrian (1872-1944).

The artist was of an extremely analytical nature:

Everything in his life was reasoned or calculated. He was a compulsive neurotic and could never bear to see anything disordered or untidy. He seemed to suffer acutely, for instance, if a table had not been laid with perfect symmetry.

— Hannah Höch on Piet Mondrian, quoted by Edouard Roditi

His pictures are typically constructed from parallel lines and rectangular areas of color, striving, it seems to me, to embrace and comprehend the infinite.

Who are we?

Mondrian is developed by Julian Hyde (project lead), Paul Dymecki, Sean McCullough, and Andreas Voss.
